About Me

Who I Am

I’m a sophomore at Dartmouth College pursuing a degree in Quantitative Social Science with a concentration in Environmental Governance, alongside minors in Government and Chemistry. I grew up in New York City and graduated from the United Nations International School in 2024 with an International Baccalaureate Diploma.

My academic work sits at the crossroads of environmental policy, international development, and data-driven social science. I’m particularly interested in how governance structures shape climate outcomes — and how policy can be designed to be both effective and equitable.

Outside the classroom, I’ve spent three summers leading wilderness expeditions for teenagers, served as a Dartmouth admissions ambassador, and built my public speaking chops in spaces ranging from Model UN to the UN General Assembly Hall, where I delivered my high school’s commencement address.

Portfolio

Selected Work

Research Environmental Policy
Environmental Governance & Climate Action in Bangladesh
Geography 8.01 — Dartmouth College, 2026

A 40-page research project examining 15+ development interventions in Bangladesh through the lens of UN Sustainable Development Goals 13 (Climate Action), 7 (Clean Energy), and 5 (Gender Equality). Evaluates solar programs, climate-resilient infrastructure, sustainable agriculture, microcredit, education, and gender governance with original policy conclusions.

Policy Op-Ed
The Solar Savings Act: Doubling New York’s Solar Tax Credit
Govt 30.16: Politics of Climate Change — Dartmouth College, 2026

A narrative op-ed and supporting policy brief proposing legislation to double New York State’s solar panel tax credit (25% → 50%, capped at $10,000), funded by a modest cannabis excise tax increase. Addresses the gap left by federal credit cuts and argues for bipartisan appeal through the lens of energy independence and financial pragmatism.

Research Political Science
Civil Rights & Development: A Global Cross-National Comparison
IB Extended Essay — United Nations International School, 2024

An extended research essay examining the relationship between civil rights protections and human development indicators across nations. Uses cross-national comparative methodology to analyze how political freedoms shape development outcomes, drawing on political science and economics literature.
